The Executive Board 2014-2015 will bring a new spin to traditional dances! If you are interested in joining the Eboard during the spring semester, feel free to contact any of the current members.

  • Sarah Lam
    Co-President

    Nutritional Sciences
    I'm a junior and have been RCDT for two years. I did Chinese Dance outside of school and I thought that this would be a great way to continue that. It takes too long to travel to my old school, so it's convenient that I can still have fun at Rutgers. I also get to learn something different being with different people. I get to meet new people and see others learn something that I have been training in for years. It is fun to see how others approach something that is outside of their comfort zone.

  • Shuang Cheng
    Co-President

    Psychology
    I'm a junior in the School of Arts and Science and have been part of RCDT for two years. I joined RCDT because I have loved Chinese dancing ever since I was little and knew I wanted to continue dancing in college. RCDT not only has helped me improve with my dancing, but it has also helped me make many new friends that I cannot live without. Besides practices, rehearsals, and performances, RCDT finds time to spend with each other and get closer. In addition, through performances, RCDT allows its members to attend many school events.

  • Audrey Dong
    Senior Advisor

    Cell Biology and Neuroscience
    My name is Audrey Dong, a senior in Cell Biology and Neuroscience. I first joined RCDT as a way to keep in touch with my culture and get some exercise, and overall to try something new. I stayed with RCDT because I really enjoyed the atmosphere. It was fun, learning new dances and attending so many events, especially since I wouldn’t have known about them otherwise. I think I also trip less often now. Overall, I hope you enjoy our performances, and feel free to join! No experience necessary!

  • Liz Tan
    Senior Advisor

    Supply Chain Management
    Hello! I am a senior at Rutgers Business School and I have been a member of RCDT since my freshman year. I joined this organization because I enjoyed learning Chinese dance at my Chinese school prior coming to Rutgers and wanted to continue in college. My past years in RCDT has help me grow as an individual, performer, and leader and I don't regret my experience! Joining this troupe is a great way to make friends, get some exercise, and try something new. Feel free to come by at one of our practices if you’re interested.

  • Leanna Bernstein
    Vice President

    Psychology
    My name is Leanna Bernstein, and I'm a junior in SAS, studying psychology. This will be my third year in RCDT, and I am glad I joined when I did. Originally, I joined because the club sounded interesting, and I wanted to learn more about Chinese culture. Upon joining, I quickly befriended people in the troupe, which is how I made a lot of friends in my freshman year. It also keeps me active during the school year, which is always a good thing.

    I am a junior majoring in ITI with a japanese minor.I joined rcdt to get involved at rutgers and get back into performing in a very safe and non stressful way. I like that we set goals together to improve our dancing and fitness, and also just hang out together.

    I'm a junior majoring in philosophy. I was invited by an acquaintance. I like RCDT because it provides a nice environment where I can explore my Chinese roots and showcase them to the world.

  • Trinh Son
    Public Relations Representative

    Information Technology
    My major is ITI with a minor in Computer Science and I am currently a junior. The reason why I decided to join RCDT is because I wanted to experience something outside of my own culture. I found that the RCDT community was very inviting which helped me adjust to Rutgers. I also love that RCDT members genuinely care about each other.

  • Catherine Rong
    Webmaster

    Information Technology
    I'm a senior and have been in RCDT since my freshman year. I joined because I wanted to try something new. RCDT is very friendly and great for any level of experience! The team is full of great people I got to know better through weekly practices, performances throughout the semester, and group outings and meals. I'm very glad to have them as friends. I hope reading this persuades you to join this family!

  • Nick Lum
    Webmaster's Apprentice

    Pharmacy
    I am a sophomore in the Ernest Mario School of Pharmacy. I was introduced to RCDT by a childhood friend who was a member of the troupe. Despite having no previous experience dancing, I quickly picked up Chinese dance with lots of help from the experienced dancers. My favorite part about RCDT is that the members of many different backgrounds come together to make a welcoming family.
