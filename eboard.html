--- layout: page title: Executive Board ---

The Executive Board 2014-2015 will bring a new spin to traditional dances! If you are interested in joining the Eboard during the spring semester, feel free to contact any of the current members.

  • Liz Tan
    President

    Supply Chain Management
    Hello! I am a junior at Rutgers Business School and I have been a member of RCDT since my freshman year. I joined this organization because I enjoyed learning Chinese dance at my Chinese school prior coming to Rutgers and wanted to continue in college. My past years in RCDT has help me grow as an individual, performer, and leader and I don't regret my experience! Joining this troupe is a great way to make friends, get some exercise, and try something new. Feel free to come by at one of our practices if you’re interested.

  • Sarah Lam
    Vice President

    Nutritional Sciences
    I'm a sophomore and have been RCDT for a year. I did Chinese Dance outside of school and I thought that this would be a great way to continue that. It takes too long to travel to my old school, so it's convenient that I can still have fun at Rutgers. I also get to learn something different being with different people. I get to meet new people and see others learn something that I have been training in for years. It is fun to see how others approach something that is outside of their comfort zone.

  • Audrey Dong
    Treasurer

    Cell Biology and Neuroscience
    My name is Audrey Dong, a junior in Cell Biology and Neuroscience. I first joined RCDT as a way to keep in touch with my culture and get some exercise, and overall to try something new. I stayed with RCDT because I really enjoyed the atmosphere. It was fun, learning new dances and attending so many events, especially since I wouldn’t have known about them otherwise. I think I also trip less often now. Overall, I hope you enjoy our performances, and feel free to join! No experience necessary!

  • Leanna Bernstein
    Secretary

    Food Science
    My name is Leanna Bernstein, and I'm a sophomore in SEBS, studying food science. This will be my second year in RCDT, and I am glad I joined when I did. Originally, I joined because the club sounded interesting, and I wanted to learn more about Chinese culture. Upon joining, I quickly befriended people in the troupe, which is how I made a lot of friends in my freshman year. It also keeps me active during the school year, which is always a good thing.

  • Shuang Cheng
    Performance Coordinator

    Undeclared
    I'm a sophomore in the School of Arts and Science and have been part of RCDT for one year. I joined RCDT because I have loved Chinese dancing ever since I was little and knew I wanted to continue dancing in college. RCDT not only has helped me improve with my dancing, but it has also helped me make many new friends that I cannot live without. Besides practices, rehearsals, and performances, RCDT finds time to spend with each other and get closer. In addition, through performances, RCDT allows its members to attend many school events.

  • Janel Borden
    Public Relations Representative

    Ecology, Evolution, and Natural Resources
    I am a Junior and this is my third year in RCDT! In the beginning of my freshman year a friend of mine who was a member of RCDT at the time vaguely told me about a "ribbon dance group" that she was apart of and invited me to come to practice with her. So one day I joined her and I've been enjoying Chinese dance ever since. RCDT is a great way to make friends, have new experiences, and get in shape all while having fun!
